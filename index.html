About

Who I Am

I am a researcher and builder at the intersection of classical Indian philosophy and modern science. My work focuses on making the depth of India's contemplative and epistemological traditions accessible — not as historical curiosities but as living frameworks for understanding the mind, consciousness, and reality.

On the technology side, I build tools that translate ancient textual knowledge into interactive, explorable formats — and tools that ground modern neuroscience data (EEG, meditation metrics) in traditional frameworks like Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras, Sāṃkhya, and the Kashmir Śaiva traditions.

My projects sit at the rare overlap between rigorous philological scholarship and practical, data-driven inquiry — a space where a ṛtambharā prajñā (truth-bearing wisdom) meets empirical measurement.

Indian Philosophy — Complete Reference
↗

An interactive reference portal for all six orthodox Indian philosophical systems (Ṣaḍdarśana) alongside the four Vedas. Each text — from Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras to Gautama's Nyāya Sūtras and the Upaniṣads — is mapped with Sanskrit originals, translations, and layered classical commentaries from 25 scholars spanning Vyāsa, Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja, Vācaspati Miśra, Udayana, and more.

Inward Journey — Meditation Analysis
↗

A neuro-philosophical dashboard for the Muse S Athena EEG headband. Upload a CSV export from the Mind Monitor app and see your session analysed through eight contemplative traditions — Yogic Citta Bhūmis, Sāṃkhya Guṇas, Patañjali's Aṣṭāṅga limbs, Kashmir Śaivism's Upāyas, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism, and Taoism — bridging brainwave data with classical meditative frameworks.
